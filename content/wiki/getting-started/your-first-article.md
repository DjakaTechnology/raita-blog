This guide takes you from a fresh install to your first generated article in under 10 minutes.

Don't have Raita yet? [Download it from the member area](https://www.raita.ai/member).

---

## Step 1: Log In

Open Raita. On the login screen, enter your email and password, then click **Sign In**.

If you don't have an account yet, click **Create account** and complete registration. You'll receive a confirmation email — click the link to activate your account.

---

## Step 2: Create a Project

After logging in you'll see the Projects screen. Click **New Project**, enter a project name (e.g. "My First Project"), and click **Create**.

A project is a container for all your articles. You can have multiple projects for different websites or niches.

![Raita main dashboard](../images/raita-main-dashboard.png)

---

## Step 3: Create Your First Article Worker

Click **+ New Task**.

Fill in:
- **Topic** — what the article should be about (e.g. "best hiking boots for beginners")
- **Niche** — your site's topic area (e.g. "outdoor gear")
- **Language** — target language (e.g. "English")

Choose a **starter template** (Simple V4 is recommended) or enter a custom prompt in the **Simple** tab. Starter templates come pre-configured — just enter your keywords and go.

![Starter template picker](../images/starting-prompt.png)

Click **Submit** to start generation.

---

## Step 4: Wait for Generation

Your worker will appear in the articles table with status **Pending**, then **Running**. Generation typically takes 30–90 seconds.

When complete, the status changes to **Success**.

![Article worker table](../images/row-actions.png)

---

## Step 5: Review the Result

Click the **View** button to open the article editor. You'll see the generated article with a rich text toolbar — adjust headings, formatting, and content as needed. Click **Save** when done.

![Article editor](../images/article-editor.png)

---

## Step 6: Export

Use the ⋮ menu and click **Bulk Export**, or click the export button on an individual row. Choose a format:

- **WordPress** — import directly into WordPress via Tools → Import
- **Blogger** — Blogger Atom format
- **Hugo/Markdown** — markdown files
- **HTML** — standalone HTML file
- **CSV** — spreadsheet format

Configure scheduling and distribution options, then click **Start Export**.

![Export dialog](../images/export-dialog.png)
